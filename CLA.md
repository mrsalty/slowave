# Contributor License Agreement

Slowave may require contributors to accept a Contributor License Agreement, or CLA, before substantial code contributions are merged.

The purpose of the CLA is to allow Slowave to remain open source while preserving the option to offer commercial licensing in the future.

Until a formal CLA process is in place:

- bug reports are welcome;
- feature requests are welcome;
- small documentation fixes may be accepted without a CLA;
- substantial code contributions may be deferred until the CLA process is ready;
- maintainers may decline or postpone large pull requests if accepting them would complicate future licensing.

By submitting a pull request, you confirm that:

- you have the right to submit the contribution;
- the contribution is your own work or is otherwise legally allowed to be contributed;
- the contribution does not knowingly include code or content you are not allowed to submit;
- you understand that substantial contributions may require CLA acceptance before merging.
